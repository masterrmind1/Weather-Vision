SO2

Sulfur dioxide (SO2), a colorless, bad-smelling, toxic gas, so2 is air pollutant composed of sulfur and oxygen. SO2 forms when sulfur-containing fuel such as coal, oil, or diesel is burned. SO2 produces acid rain Sulfur dioxide can contribute to respiratory illness by making breathing more difficult, especially for children, the elderly, and those with pre-existing conditions. Longer exposures can aggravate existing heart and lung conditions, as well. alsoaffects the respiratory system, particularly lung function, and can irritate the eyes. Sulfur dioxide irritates the respiratory tract and increases the risk of tract infections.
